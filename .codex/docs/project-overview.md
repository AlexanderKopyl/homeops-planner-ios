# HomeOps Planner — Project Overview

## What HomeOps Planner is

HomeOps Planner is an iOS-only local-first app for managing recurring household operations in one place.

The app helps a single user keep track of household supplies, recurring maintenance tasks, preferred purchase links, service providers, and pending actions.

## Problems it solves

Household operations are usually scattered across memory, notes, chats, browser bookmarks, calendars, and ad-hoc reminders.

HomeOps Planner centralizes this information so the user can quickly understand:

- what supplies are running low;
- what maintenance needs attention soon;
- where a specific item is usually purchased;
- who to contact for a specific maintenance task;
- what actions are pending now.

## Core product questions

The product should answer these questions clearly:

1. What is running low soon?
2. What needs to be done soon?
3. Where do I usually buy this?
4. Who do I contact for this?
5. What actions are pending now?

## Version 1 validation goal

Version 1 exists to validate whether the product is useful as a simple local-first iOS app before investing in backend, accounts, sync, sharing, Android, or cloud infrastructure.

The first version should prove that the user can create, maintain, and act on household operational data without a server.

## Core product areas

### Home dashboard

A compact overview of due-soon tasks, running-low supplies, and pending actions.

### Supplies

Household consumables such as filters, detergent, batteries, dishwasher tablets, light bulbs, or cleaning products.

### Maintenance tasks

Recurring tasks such as boiler maintenance, AC cleaning, replacing filters, calling a technician, or checking home equipment.

### Merchants

Preferred purchase links or stores for supplies.

### Service providers

Contacts for people or companies used for maintenance work.

### Task / buy list

A practical list of actions the user should take now or soon.

## Version 1 boundary reminder

Version 1 is local-only, iOS-only, single-user, and does not include backend, accounts, sync, Android, Firebase, or family sharing.
